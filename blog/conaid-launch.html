Introducing CONAID: Post-Bid Contract Management, Built for Civil Contractors

Winning the bid is the easy part. What happens after — BOQ progress, RA billing, payment milestones, compliance deadlines — is where projects actually get won or lost.

At Phoenixtek, we built CONAID because we needed it ourselves. Running geotechnical and structural monitoring projects across India means juggling awarded contracts across multiple authorities, each with its own BOQ, billing cycle, and compliance paperwork — and we were doing it the way most civil contractors still do: across ten different spreadsheets per project, hoping nothing slips through.

It did slip through, eventually. A bank guarantee lapsed without anyone noticing until it became a problem. That's the moment CONAID stopped being a nice-to-have and became something we had to build. Now we're bringing it to other civil contractors who are managing the same chaos.

What it does

  • ✓
    Contract RegisterLOA, Award Date, Authority Engineer, DLP, Time of Completion — every detail in one place, not scattered across files.
  • ✓
    BOQ-Linked Progress TrackingReal completion percentage, rolled up per item and per contract — not guesswork.
  • ✓
    RA BillingCumulative billing validated against BOQ quantities, so you never over-bill or lose sight of what's certified versus paid.
  • ✓
    Payment MilestonesPercentage-based tracking from mobilization advance to final handover.
  • ✓
    Document & Compliance AlertsBank guarantees, EMD, insurance, and EOTs tracked with expiry warnings — before they become a crisis.
  • ✓
    Material Planning MatrixKnow what materials — stone, cement, bitumen, steel — each work type actually needs: WMM, DBM, BT, Bridge, CD, and more.
  • ✓
    Excel Import, EverywhereBOQ, milestones, material data — upload straight from the sheets your team already works in.

Built for how contractors actually work

CONAID isn't trying to replace the way your team already tracks things — it's trying to stop that tracking from living in ten different files that nobody fully trusts. Every awarded contract gets one record: the register details, the BOQ, the billing history, the milestones, and the compliance clock, all connected instead of copy-pasted between spreadsheets.

That connection is what makes RA billing safer — the system checks cumulative billing against BOQ quantities automatically, so an over-claim gets caught before it goes out, not after an audit. And it's what makes the compliance alerts actually useful: instead of a calendar reminder someone has to remember to set, expiry warnings on bank guarantees, EMDs, and insurance surface on their own.

No more ten spreadsheets per project. No more finding out a bank guarantee lapsed after the fact.
